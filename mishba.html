Why MISHBA

MISHBA is a fast-casual Berlin burrito chain known for its affordable prices, fresh ingredients, customisable burritos and bowls, and vegan-friendly menu. It has multiple locations across the city and serves a primarily student and young professional crowd.

This project began not from an assignment brief, but from a genuine personal experience. MISHBA became my comfort food when I first arrived in Berlin as a Master's student — a familiar, affordable, and satisfying meal in a new city. That personal connection made me pay close attention to what the brand does well, and where it falls short.

The biggest gap I noticed was packaging. The current foil and paper wrapping is messy when eaten on the go, doesn't retain heat well, offers no branding surface, and generates unnecessary waste. This project set out to fix that — with a solution that works for the customer, the staff, and the brand.

Technology Research
Three Technologies That Could Transform MISHBA's Operations
1
Smart Packaging — QR Code Integration
QR code-enabled packaging allows customers to scan for real-time nutritional information, ingredient sourcing, allergen data, and loyalty programmes. Reduces paper label usage while increasing brand engagement and customer trust. Already adopted by major food brands as a sustainability-aligned solution.
2
AI-Driven Plant-Based Innovation
Companies like NotCo use AI (their "Giuseppe" model) to replicate animal products at the molecular level using plant-based ingredients. For MISHBA, this opens a path to expanding its already strong vegan menu with products that match the taste and texture of meat options — a major market differentiator in Berlin's vegan-forward market.
3
IoT Waste Reduction (Kitro Technology)
Kitro is an AI-powered IoT food waste monitoring system — a camera above the bin and a scale below capture what is thrown away in real time. This data helps restaurant operators reduce portion waste and make data-driven purchasing decisions. For a chain like MISHBA with multiple locations, this could meaningfully reduce operational costs.
The Solution
Hexagonal Biodegradable Packaging with Banana Peel Opening
Prototype concept — hexagonal biodegradable kraft box with perforated edges for the "banana peel" eating mechanism

After evaluating multiple packaging formats (cylindrical tubes, standard rectangular boxes, hexagonal boxes), the hexagonal kraft box was selected using a ranking and prioritisation method based on criteria including sustainability, usability, branding potential, and manufacturing feasibility.

①
Biodegradable Material
Made from Finland's fibre-based packaging movement — eliminates the current paper-foil approach with a fully compostable alternative.
②
Banana Peel Opening
Perforations along the hexagonal edges allow the packaging to be peeled open like a banana as the burrito is eaten — eliminating the mess of foil wrapping.
③
Insulated & Spill-Proof
The hexagonal shape provides structural integrity, heat retention, and moisture resistance — keeping burritos warm and intact during takeout and delivery.
④
Branding + QR Surface
Flat outer surfaces provide space for MISHBA's logo, nutritional information, and QR codes — converting every order into a brand touchpoint.
⑤
Stackable & Space-Saving
The geometric design allows boxes to stack efficiently in the kitchen — improving storage and reducing preparation time for staff.
Stakeholder Testing
Real Feedback from MISHBA Staff and Customers

The prototype concept (presented as an image) was tested directly with two distinct groups — MISHBA outlet workers and regular MISHBA customers — using structured interview questions. This two-group approach captured both operational and consumer perspectives.

👷
Feedback from MISHBA Outlet Workers
Ease of packing — workers found the prototype would be simple to seal, reducing preparation time.

Storage efficiency — the stackable design was highly appreciated compared to foil rolls.

Structural stability — the hexagonal form was rated positively for both packing ease and leak-proof characteristics.
🙋
Feedback from MISHBA Customers
First impression — customers perceived the new packaging as premium and higher quality than current wrapping.

Handling & eating — the structured shape was seen as significantly better for on-the-go eating without spillage.

Freshness & heat — customers agreed the design would keep burritos warmer for longer — a major benefit for delivery orders.
Implementation Plan
Three-Phase Rollout Strategy
1
Phase 1 — Concept Testing
Present the image-based prototype to MISHBA customers and outlet workers. Gather structured feedback on customer acceptance rate, operational impact, and cost-effectiveness. Define final package dimensions, material specifications, and key requirements before moving to physical prototyping.
2
Phase 2 — Pilot Testing
Produce limited physical samples and test in select MISHBA outlet locations. Collect feedback from staff and customers using the real product. Evaluate acceptance rate, worker handling time, storage efficiency, and sustainability metrics. Make any final design adjustments before scaling.
3
Phase 3 — Full-Scale Implementation
Roll out finalised packaging across all MISHBA outlets. Launch marketing campaign highlighting the new sustainable packaging. Measure customer satisfaction through post-purchase surveys, operational efficiency improvements, and sustainability goals (waste reduction, supply chain efficiency).
