Product Design · User Research
IKEA 365+ Bottle Cap Redesign
Identified a real user pain from IKEA customer reviews and designed a low-cost cap improvement through competitor analysis, TinkerCAD prototyping and two structured rounds of user testing.
2025
TinkerCAD User Testing Prototyping
IKEA 365+ bottle — the product at the centre of this redesign project
TinkerCAD full prototype — bottle and redesigned cap assembly
Prototype close-up — silicone ring, retention ridge and cap components
The Problem

The silicone sealing ring on the IKEA 365+ reusable water bottle (€3.99) frequently falls out during normal use or dishwashing, causing leaks and making the bottle unusable — with no replacement parts available. Identified through systematic analysis of real IKEA customer reviews.

What I Did
  • Analysed verified IKEA customer reviews to define the core user problem
  • Conducted competitor analysis across EMSA, Contigo and Quechua bottles
  • Formed hypothesis: deeper groove + retention ridge = seal stays in place
  • Built digital prototype in TinkerCAD (learned the tool for this project)
  • Ran 2 structured test rounds — 7 classmates at Hochschule Fresenius + 5 Flink colleagues
  • Iterated to Prototype 2 based on feedback, maintaining IKEA's low-cost philosophy
  • Prepared executive recommendation for IKEA CEO as Executive Development Officer

Arch MAX — Customizable Football Boots
Co-founded a startup concept targeting 1.3M flat-footed football players in Germany. Served as CFO, building a complete financial model from unit economics to an 18-month P&L, break-even analysis and full investor pitch.
2024
Financial Modelling 3D Printing Startup Strategy
Arch MAX — "Fit It Your Way" · startup concept for 3D-customised football boots
The problem — flat feet cause 50% higher injury risk for 1.3M German football players
Product concept — fully customisable football boot with 3D-printed insoles
18-month P&L — break-even Month 12 · Total profit €751,989
The Problem

20% of all humans have flat feet. In Germany, approximately 1.3 million football players are affected, facing 50% higher injury risk. All existing solutions (VKTRY €200+, Stride Soles €149+) are non-customisable and sold separately from shoes. No affordable, personalised, integrated product exists in the European market.

My Role as CFO
  • Built complete CapEx/OpEx model — €1.87M total investment over 18 months
  • Cost per unit: €46.73 with 45% profit margin → selling price €79.99
  • TAM/SAM/TOM analysis: 7M → 1.75M → 40,000 target units
  • Full 18-month P&L with monthly EBIT and cumulative cash flow
  • Break-even: Month 12 at ~27,808 units · Total 18-month profit: €751,989
  • Full Business Model Canvas across 9 key dimensions
  • Delivered complete investor pitch deck

Engineering · Automation · Sustainability
Automated Waste Segregation System
Designed, programmed and physically fabricated a working automated waste segregator using Arduino UNO, a conveyor belt and multiple sensors — demonstrated as a fully operational prototype at the Bachelor's final review.
2022
Arduino Fabrication Embedded C
Engineering design drawing — top and front view of the fabricated waste segregator system
The Problem

India produces 62 million tonnes of municipal solid waste per year — only 20% gets treated. Manual waste separation in industries is slow, hazardous and inefficient. A low-cost, automated segregation system was needed for urban and industrial deployment.

What We Built
  • Designed and physically fabricated a conveyor belt system from scratch
  • Programmed Arduino UNO microcontroller in Embedded C
  • IR sensor activates conveyor belt upon waste detection
  • Capacitive sensor identifies dry waste (plastic, paper) → Bin 1
  • Moisture sensor detects wet waste → Bin 2
  • Inductive proximity sensor identifies metallic waste → Bin 3
  • Bin-full monitoring system alerts authorities automatically
  • Demonstrated as a working physical prototype at final review
