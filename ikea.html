IKEA 365+ Bottle Cap Redesign

A hypothesis-driven redesign of the IKEA 365+ reusable water bottle cap, addressing seal dislodgement through competitor research, TinkerCAD prototyping, and two rounds of structured user testing.

The Problem

The IKEA 365+ reusable water bottle (€3.99) is one of IKEA's most popular everyday products. However, systematic analysis of verified customer reviews revealed a persistent and fundamental flaw: the silicone sealing ring frequently falls out during normal use or dishwashing cycles, causing the bottle to leak and rendering it unusable.

Compounding the problem, IKEA offers no replacement sealing rings for purchase, meaning customers are forced to buy an entirely new bottle when the seal fails — contradicting the very sustainability argument for using a reusable bottle in the first place.

As Executive Development Officer on this project, the goal was to identify a low-cost, practical improvement that stays true to IKEA's core design philosophy: simplicity, affordability, and usability.

Selected problem statement: "How might we improve the sealing mechanism and make sure the seal stays in its place to avoid leakage?" — chosen because it was the most-reported issue with a direct impact on basic product functionality.

Research & Process
From Customer Reviews to Competitor Analysis

The project began with a structured review of real IKEA customer feedback, which identified three recurring complaints: seal dislodgement during use or washing, no availability of spare sealing rings, and occasional colour mismatch between the product and online images.

This was followed by a competitor analysis benchmarking the IKEA bottle against three market alternatives to understand how other brands solve the same sealing challenge:

🔵
EMSA Drink2Go (€10–15)
Twist cap with integrated silicone seal and replaceable components. Strong sealing but significantly more expensive and bulkier than IKEA.
🟢
Contigo AUTOSEAL™ (€15–25)
Patented self-sealing button mechanism — no removable parts means no seal loss. Extremely leak-proof but high price and complex mechanism.
🟡
Quechua ECOZEN / Decathlon (€2.99)
Deep-threaded screw cap providing stronger sealing through geometry rather than a separate seal ring. Closest to IKEA in price, key insight for the redesign.
💡
Key Insight
The most affordable solution to seal retention is mechanical — deeper grooves and retention structures — not expensive materials or complex mechanisms.
Solution Hypothesis & Prototype
Deeper Groove + Retention Ridge

Hypothesis: "If the bottle cap includes a deeper groove and a retention ridge, the silicone seal will stay in place more securely, significantly reducing leakage incidents."

The intervention was deliberately minimal — two small structural changes to the existing cap design:

1
Deeper Groove
The internal groove where the silicone ring sits was deepened, eliminating the chance of the ring slipping or dislodging during cleaning or normal use.
2
Retaining Ridge (Lip)
A thin circular lip added directly above the groove acts as a mechanical barrier, stopping upward movement of the silicone ring during high-pressure sealing or dishwasher cycles.
3
TinkerCAD Digital Model
The redesigned cap was modelled in TinkerCAD — a tool learned specifically for this project — allowing the design to be visualised and shared with test participants without requiring physical manufacturing.

User Testing
Two Structured Rounds with Different User Groups

To validate the design before recommending it to IKEA, two rounds of structured user testing were conducted using a prepared testing guide with consistent open-ended questions across both sessions.

🏫
Round 1 — 7 Classmates at Hochschule Fresenius
15-minute classroom session on prototype day. Participants were shown the TinkerCAD digital prototype and answered structured questions. All 7 participants understood the model immediately. All agreed the redesign would solve the leakage issue. One participant raised a cost concern — addressed by explaining that only minor tooling changes are needed, with no additional materials or significant cost increase.
🏭
Round 2 — 5 Flink Colleagues
10-minute session at the Flink workplace with colleagues who have daily experience using reusable bottles. All 5 participants confirmed the design would solve the leakage problem. One participant asked whether the deeper groove would increase cap height — this led directly to the creation of Prototype 2, which highlighted the height extension visually.
